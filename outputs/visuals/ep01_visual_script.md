# EP01 Visual Script

## Episode

- ID: `ep01`
- Title: `The Source of Everything`
- Video mode: `non-face visual podcast`
- Goal: help the viewer understand Yin and Yang as a practical framework, not a distant philosophy lesson

## Visual Strategy

This is not a fake talking-head video.
This is a guided visual essay built from:

- title cards
- keyword subtitles
- simple diagrams
- real-life mood scenes
- selective Seedance hero shots
- chapter transitions

Seedance is used for:
- opening impact
- symbolic transitions
- short clips

Seedance is not used to carry the entire long video.

## Global Visual Rules

- Every 20-40 seconds: noticeable visual change
- Every 60-90 seconds: at least one information layer
- Abstract narration must be paired with one of:
  - real-life scene
  - keyword card
  - diagram
  - contrast graphic
- Keep subtitles selective, not full-screen transcript walls

## Segment 1

### Title
Hook: Life Is Not a Rock

### Time
`00:00-00:25`

### Narration Goal
Open with a tension: people think life is fixed, but life is actually patterned movement.

### Viewer Takeaway
This episode matters because the listener's stress and confusion are connected to how they relate to change.

### Visual Function
- grab attention
- establish tone
- promise relevance

### Visual Direction
- fast title card
- one strong Seedance shot of moving water / ripple / shifting light
- large on-screen question
- brief cut to modern-life scenes

### On-Screen Text
- `What if life is not stable, but rhythmic?`
- `Why do we suffer when we fight the pattern?`

## Segment 2

### Title
The Real Problem: We Keep Forcing What Should Be Timed

### Time
`00:25-01:40`

### Narration Goal
Translate the ancient topic into a current pain point: over-control, overthinking, pushing at the wrong moment.

### Viewer Takeaway
The topic is not abstract philosophy. It is about everyday misalignment.

### Visual Direction
- keyword subtitles
- real-life scenes of texting anxiety, overwork, decision fatigue
- split-screen contrast: pushing vs pausing

### On-Screen Text
- `overthinking`
- `forcing`
- `bad timing`
- `trying to control everything`

## Segment 3

### Title
Yin and Yang as Living Patterns

### Time
`01:40-04:30`

### Narration Goal
Explain Yin and Yang in plain English: not opposites at war, but complementary movement inside one living process.

### Viewer Takeaway
Balance is dynamic, not static.

### Visual Direction
- animated line graphics
- simple diagram: expansion vs contraction
- day/night, inhale/exhale, effort/rest visual pairing
- very short symbolic Seedance transition

### On-Screen Text
- `not enemies`
- `one process, two movements`
- `expansion / contraction`
- `effort / receptivity`

## Segment 4

### Title
What Imbalance Looks Like in Real Life

### Time
`04:30-08:30`

### Narration Goal
Show where Yin/Yang imbalance appears today: relationships, burnout, indecision, emotional exhaustion.

### Viewer Takeaway
The viewer should recognize themselves in at least one example.

### Visual Direction
- 3 real-life case blocks
- each block starts with a title card
- each block uses relatable mood scenes

### Case Block A
`Relationships`
- `too much force breaks connection`
- `too much retreat creates distance`

### Case Block B
`Work and burnout`
- `constant output is not strength`
- `rest is not weakness`

### Case Block C
`Decision-making`
- `clarity is not control`
- `timing matters more than pressure`

## Segment 5

### Title
Practical Application

### Time
`08:30-12:00`

### Narration Goal
Move from understanding to use. Give the viewer a way to apply the idea tonight.

### Viewer Takeaway
The episode becomes useful, not just insightful.

### Visual Direction
- step cards
- notebook / journaling visuals
- calm real-world footage

### On-Screen Text
- `Ask: Am I forcing or listening?`
- `Where am I pushing past the right timing?`
- `What would balance look like here?`

### Suggested 3-Step Card
1. Notice where tension is repeating
2. Name the movement: push or withdraw
3. Adjust by one step, not ten

## Segment 6

### Title
Closing Reflection

### Time
`12:00-13:00` or final minute of the edited episode

### Narration Goal
End with one memorable line and a feeling of grounded insight.

### Viewer Takeaway
Balance is not perfection. It is intelligent response to the moment.

### Visual Direction
- slower mood footage
- one final Seedance hero shot
- large text close

### On-Screen Text
- `Balance is not 50/50.`
- `Balance is knowing what the moment asks for.`
- `Full episode in description / follow for the next layer.`

If the visuals ever become prettier than they are useful, simplify them.
The job of this video is to make the audio easier to understand, easier to feel, and easier to remember.
